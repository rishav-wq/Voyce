# Voyce — First-90-Days Content & Conversion Playbook
*(Deep-research verified, July 2026. 20 sources, 25 claims 3-vote verified, 11 refuted.)*

## The reality check (verified numbers, calibrate expectations to these)

| Founder | What happened | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Tibo (Tweet Hunter → Taplio, sold ~$10M) | First paying customer May 2021 → $1.5K MRR July → $3K Sept | **~4 months of grinding to $3K** |
| Tony Dinh | 9-10 months of daily build-in-public → 8,000 followers, **$500/mo total** | Audience grows faster than revenue |
| Tony Dinh's $22K week | Came from a launch tweet to **76K followers built over 2.5 years** | Not a 90-day outcome |
| Tweet Hunter's $3K→$20K jump | Gave influencer J.K. Molina **up to 25% equity** to launch to his audience | Borrowed audience, not posting volume |

**Day-90 success for Voyce = single-digit paying customers (~5-25 at ₹4,199) and a visibly
more founder-heavy follower base.** Anyone promising faster is selling a course.

## What verifiably converts (in order of evidence strength)

1. **Milestone / honest-metrics posts** — the ONLY documented post→named-customer chain in
   the entire evidence base: Damon Chen tweeted "approaching $1,000 MRR" → Andrew Gazdecki
   (Acquire.com) saw it and bought the top plan. Negative numbers count as milestones too
   ("50 DMs, 4 replies, 0 customers").
2. **Free mini-tools / lead magnets** — Tibo shipped a free generator ~monthly as the signup
   engine (verified tactic; note: at scale, extrapolated to early stage).
3. **Launch posts to a borrowed audience** — every inflection in the data required either a
   distribution partner or a huge pre-built audience.

## What verifiably fails

- **Pure product-pitch posts.** Tony Dinh, verbatim: "tweeting about DevUtils alone wasn't
  going to help. People just don't care." Follower growth started only when he switched to
  building interesting things in public + engaging + humor.
- **Reach without conversion.** ContentIn ran a coordinated viral campaign (free scheduler,
  influencer boosts): "It massively failed... a little blip of traffic, almost no leads."
  Likes from builders measure the post, not the product.
- **The 95:5 rule** (Ehrenberg-Bass/LinkedIn B2B Institute): ~95% of any audience is not
  in-market. Content must be ~95% trust-building, ~5% pitch. Build-in-public metrics posts
  are the sanctioned way to "pitch" — they read as story, not ad.

## Corrections to our own prior advice

- **"Link in comments" has NO verified data behind it.** The Hootsuite link-penalty numbers
  and the Agorapulse link-in-comments stats both failed 3-vote verification. Nobody reliably
  knows. Do whichever is convenient; stop optimizing this.
- Featured-section-as-landing-page: also unverified convention. Set it up (free, 5 min), but
  expect nothing measurable from it.

## The weekly system (weeks 1-4: cadence + audience migration)

4-5 posts/week:
- **2× value/teaching** posts aimed at FOUNDERS (not engineers) — LinkedIn growth lessons,
  outreach lessons, honest SaaS mechanics
- **1-2× build-in-public metrics** posts — real numbers, including zeros and failures
- **1× meta-dogfooding artifact** — "Voyce wrote this post" with the rough-input → output
  shown. Nobody else can make this post; it is simultaneously proof and demo
- **≤1 direct pitch per 2 weeks**

Daily, non-negotiable: **20 min commenting on founder-ICP posts** (the audience-migration
lever — 2.4K engineer followers won't buy; comments in founder rooms recruit the right ones)
+ golden hour on own posts.

## Weeks 5-8: add the signup engine

- **Ship one free mini-tool**: a LinkedIn Hook Generator (paste topic → 5 hooks in your
  tone) is the obvious one — tiny slice of Voyce's existing engine, useful standalone,
  every output advertises the full product. This becomes the thing posts/comments can link
  to without feeling like a pitch.
- **Line up ONE borrowed-audience play**: a micro-creator partnership (10-50K founder-ICP
  creator; offer free Pro + rev-share/affiliate, or a genuine collab), and/or a proper
  Peerlist Launchpad launch. Posting volume alone verifiably does not inflect.

## Weeks 9-12: launch push + judge with data

- Product Hunt (with testimonials collected by then), second borrowed-audience play,
  double down on whichever archetype's numbers led.
- Judge against the verified benchmark: low hundreds of $/mo equivalent and a follower base
  that now contains actual founders = ON TRACK. Zero activated users after honestly running
  this = revisit ICP/positioning with real data.

## Caveats the research itself flags
Self-reported numbers, survivorship bias, mostly X/Twitter 2021-2023 evidence (LinkedIn
specifics are thin), and the mini-tool cadence is a scale-stage tactic extrapolated
backward. Treat this as the best available map, not gospel.
