# Voyce — Step 1: ICP, Positioning & Differentiation

**Date:** 2026-07-22 · **Status:** DECIDED (pending items in §6)
**Verdict on current hypothesis:** The ICP hypothesis **survives** — consultants/fractional/coaches rank #1 on pain × pay × reachability. Two sharpenings the research forces: (1) the beachhead-within-the-beachhead is **fractional executives**, not the broad consultant/coach pool; (2) the *headline* cannot be "zero-touch, no approval, ever" — every adjacent market (AI SDRs, autonomous ads) shows full-autonomy headlines trigger churn and distrust in brand-sensitive contexts ([UnifyGTM](https://www.unifygtm.com/explore/ai-copilot-vs-autonomous-ai-sdr), [Digiday](https://digiday.com/marketing/trust-becomes-the-product-marketers-grapple-with-googles-new-suite-of-ai-powered-ad-agents/)). Sell delegation with a kill switch; ship the approval queue before leaning harder on autonomy.

## 1. Positioning statement

### Primary — "The AI ghostwriter that actually posts" (existing category + differentiator, price-anchored to the human service)

> **For** solo B2B consultants, fractional executives, and coaches who get clients from LinkedIn but can't sustain posting, **Voyce is an AI LinkedIn ghostwriter** that — **unlike** Taplio, Supergrow, and every draft-queue tool — finds the day's news in your niche, writes your take in your voice, and **publishes it on schedule while you're with clients**. A human ghostwriter who does this costs $1,000–$5,000/mo ([Foundera](https://www.foundera.co/blog/linkedin-ghostwriting-pricing-guide-2026)); Voyce is $29/mo, with a full activity log, preview mode, and one-click pause — via LinkedIn's official API.

Why this one: "AI LinkedIn ghostwriter" is an existing, searched category (ContentIn, TeamPost, Oiti, Postiv already validate it); the differentiator ("it hits publish") is instantly graspable; and the price anchor is the $1–5K human service — not Supergrow's $19 — which puts Voyce at 1/20th–1/100th instead of $10 more expensive.

### Fallback — category creation: "Your LinkedIn presence, fully autonomous"

> Voyce is the first autonomous LinkedIn content system: 3-minute setup, then it posts every day — finds the news, writes in your voice, publishes on time — whether or not you show up. Every post logged with its news source; pause anytime.

Hold in reserve until there are retained customers with pipeline testimonials. Category creation is expensive for a solo founder, "autopilot" is polluted by spam-adjacent tools (~40% of accounts using non-compliant automation restricted Jan–Mar 2026 — [LinkBoost](https://www.linkboost.co/blog/how-to-automate-linkedin-posts-2026)), and the whole sale collapses into one objection: "what if it posts something wrong under my name?"

### "Only we" claims that survived research

Killed as differentiators: voice learning ("table stakes by 2026" — [ConnectSafely](https://connectsafely.ai/articles/best-kleo-alternative-linkedin-content-creation-2026)), official LinkedIn API (ViralBrain, Autoposting.ai all claim it), carousels (Supergrow at $19), price (Supergrow is cheaper). What's left:

1. **Only software that closes the loop on *today's* news, unattended.** Same-day niche news (live Tavily search) → voice-matched post → published, zero keystrokes. ViralBrain auto-publishes but from a pre-approved evergreen 30-day queue — it structurally cannot react to yesterday's news; Autoposting.ai is multi-platform and dev-facing. Nobody else markets "it posted about today's news in your niche while you were with clients."
2. **"Replaces the habit, not just the writing."** Every software rival sells faster writing; the real #1 competitor is habit failure ("nothing / sporadic posting"). Voyce is the only tool whose promise is the *elimination of the daily decision* — this framing is unclaimed across the entire market.
3. **Provenance per post.** Full activity log citing the news source each post reacted to, plus dry-run mode and one-click pause. No competitor markets an audit trail for autonomous posting — and since "what if it posts something wrong" is the category's #1 objection, this is the conversion mechanism, not a nice-to-have.
4. **Ghostwriter outcome at ~1/35th the median price.** The only alternative that also removes the user from the loop is a human at $1,000–$5,000/mo — and even they demand interview/call time. Voyce: $29/mo, 3-minute one-time setup.
5. *(Fragile — hold lightly)* **Only auto-publisher at $29.** ViralBrain's auto-publish tier is €69. But the moat here is one feature flag deep for them; do not build positioning on price alone.

**Standing caveat:** the autonomy gap is a positioning gap, not a technical one. Durable moat = trust infrastructure (provenance, approval queue, dry-run) + post quality that survives LinkedIn's AI-slop reach penalty + owning the consultant outcome metric (client conversations, not impressions) before Meet Sona does.

## 4. Anti-ICP — who we say no to

- **Control-oriented writers** who enjoy writing and want editing help — AuthoredUp/EasyGen serve them; autonomy is a bug to them, not a feature.
- **Personal-story-heavy creators** — autonomy cannot source lived experience ("one real datapoint, one honest opinion, one scar" — [Hiration](https://www.hiration.com/blog/ai-slop-linkedin/)); news-reaction formats will underserve them and generate churn + slop accusations.
- **Corporate/regulated executives & employed salespeople** — employer compliance requires review of every post; unreviewed autonomous publishing is a disqualifier, and the buyer is the company, not the individual.
- **Agencies (today)** — cannot publish to client profiles without sign-off; say "not yet" until the approval queue + white-label reporting exist.
- **Non-LinkedIn SMBs** — their audience is on Facebook/Instagram; Voyce is LinkedIn-only.
- **Growth hackers wanting engagement automation** — no auto-DMs, auto-connects, or pods, ever; that's the ban-risk category we position against.

## 6. Open questions (testable assumptions)

1. **Zero-touch vs one-tap approval.** Assumption: "autopilot + optional daily approval tap" converts better than pure zero-touch for this reputation-sensitive ICP. Test in validation interviews and with the approval-queue build order. All evidence (AI-SDR precedent, slop backlash, every competitor keeping a human gate) points one way; confirm before making no-touch the headline.
2. **Daily vs 3–5x/week cadence.** The best proof-point consultant posts 2x/week and gets 80% of clients from LinkedIn. Assumption: offering 3–5x/week options reduces slop-fear and churn vs "daily." Test with cadence selector + retention data.
3. **Can unattended news-reaction posts clear the "one honest opinion, one scar" bar?** This is the existential quality question — LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm reportedly reduces reach of low-effort AI text ([HeyReach](https://www.heyreach.io/blog/best-linkedin-automation-tools)). Test: measure reach/engagement of autopilot posts vs the user's manual baseline over 14 posts; make the authority-context/opinion input a marketed feature ("your take, on autopilot").
4. **Pricing inconsistencies must be resolved before outreach.** ₹2,499 (live site) vs $29 (pitch) vs ₹1,599 (playbook benchmark). Evidence supports ~₹1,499–1,699 India / $29 global ([Playto](https://www.playto.so/blogs/how-to-price-your-saas-for-indian-vs-international-customers-in-2026)). Decision owner: founder, this week.
5. **Raw ICP verbatims are thin from Reddit.** r/consulting and r/freelance were poorly indexed; quotes lean on Blind/Trustpilot/Substack. Do 5–10 manual Reddit searches or the first 10 customer interviews before locking manifesto copy.
6. **"Rival went viral" and "New Year restart" triggers** — no verbatim evidence found; treat as hypotheses, do not build messaging on them yet.
7. **How fast does ViralBrain (or Autoposting.ai) add a news feed?** The moat is one feature flag deep. Assumption: 1–2 quarters. Mitigation to validate: trust layer + ICP outcome metrics (client conversations booked, not impressions) as the durable wedge.
