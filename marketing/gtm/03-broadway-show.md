# Voyce — Step 3: The Broadway Show (30-Day Distribution Plan)

**Date:** 2026-07-22 · **Status:** OPERATING PLAN
**Sources:** `marketing/gtm/02-manifesto.md` (ratified narrative — every post and DM uses its language), `marketing/gtm/01-icp.md` (fractional-exec beachhead + language bank), `marketing/content-playbook.md` (verified benchmarks — deltas noted in §6), `PITCH.md` (product truth), `marketing/validation-sprint.md` (concierge scripts this plan instruments).

> **The budget rule this plan obeys:** the three channel drafts summed to ~18.5 founder-hours/week. A plan that doesn't fit a solo founder's week is fiction, so this document is the reconciled version at **~8 h/week**. What got cut: all double-counted time (one commenting routine now serves both organic and outbound), all founder-written daily posts (Voyce writes them — that's the dogfood), and the validation-sprint's 80-cold-openers-per-month funnel, which violated the outbound ladder's own hard caps (≤5 first-touch DMs/week). ## 1. The show in one line

**Your pipeline dies from silence, not from bad writing — so every channel performs the same act: Voyce keeps Rishav visible, in his voice, while he's doing other work, and the audience watches it happen live.**

The feed is the demo. The DMs are the customer interviews. The scoreboard is next week's post. One motion, three views of it. Every post and every message reinforces the primary message — **"The AI ghostwriter that actually posts"** — using the manifesto's word bank (showing up, going quiet, the blank composer, while you're with clients, sounds like me, pipeline) and never a banned word (no "automation," no "content at scale," no "zero-touch," no uncited stat — the product's fabrication guard enforces in code the same rule the manifesto enforces in copy).

### Engagement routine — 15 min/day, non-negotiable, shared with outbound

This is the audience-migration lever: Rishav's ~2.4K existing followers are engineers who won't buy; comments recruit the right room. Aimed at **fractional execs and consultants** (founders convert only if they arrive on their own — ICP §2). This same routine IS rung 1 of the outbound ladder (§3) — the time is counted once.

**One-time setup (Day 0, two ~25-min blocks):** (a) *Prospect list:* 25 fractional executives via LinkedIn title search ("Fractional CMO/CFO/COO/CTO"), active posters, 2nd-degree preferred. (b) *Watering-hole list:* 8–10 creators who teach consultants/fractionals how to run their business — their comment sections are dense with the exact buyer. Refresh both ~15 min weekly; drop anyone inactive.

| Minutes | What | Why them |
|---|---|---|
| 0–5 | Golden hour on Rishav's own post: reply to every comment | Algorithm reward + every commenter is a warm name for the DM ladder |
| 5–11 | 3 substantive comments on prospect-list posts (posted <24h ago) | These people ARE the buyer; a real comment makes any future DM non-cold — this is ladder rung 1 |
| 11–15 | 1–2 comments on watering-hole creator posts | One good comment is seen by hundreds of ICP readers — highest visibility-per-minute at $0 |

**Comment quality bar = the post bar:** one real datapoint, one honest opinion, or one scar. Never "Great post!", never a pitch, never a link. Weekends: skip golden hour (no post), spend the full 15 on the prospect list.

**Weekly cost:** engagement routine 15 min × 7 = 1.75 h · arm/review the day's autopilot post ~5 min × 5 = 0.4 h · seeded/hand-written post (about one per week: raw input to the manual tool, or a reveal draft) ~0.75 h → **~3 h/week.**

## 3. Outbound — the warm DM ladder, by hand, forever (~4.5 h/week, of which 1.5 h is the shared commenting above)

**Rule zero:** all of this is manual, permanently. Voyce positions *against* engagement automation ("no auto-DMs, no auto-connects, no pods, ever"). One screenshot of an automated Voyce founder DM kills the brand. Manual outbound isn't a limitation — it's the product ethics, demonstrated.

**Second job:** the manifesto (§6.10) is frozen pending 10 customer interviews. Every ladder conversation IS that interview pipeline. Weeks 1–6 of this channel are scored on *conversations and verbatims collected*, not signups.

### Prospect definition (ICP §2)

A rung-ready prospect is a fractional executive who:
- Has "Fractional CMO / CFO / COO / CTO / CHRO" (or Fractional-anything) in headline or title — the searchable beachhead (~110K profiles)
- Sells B2B services solo or near-solo (no marketing team visible)
- Shows LinkedIn matters to them: has posted, 500+ connections, talks about clients/pipeline
- **Shows the burst-and-silence pattern** — the highest-intent signal in the ICP. Their activity tab is the qualification tool.

**Priority stack (best first):** 1) burst-poster now silent 3+ weeks · 2) just announced going fractional ("first client in two weeks" energy) · 3) publicly complained about content pressure, ghostwriter cost, or a dry pipeline · 4) sporadic poster with visible effort ("been quiet lately…" apology posts).

**Expansion order when the fractional well runs dry:** solo B2B consultants → coaches. Founders are never targeted — convert them only inbound.

### Sourcing — 10 new prospects/week, two 30-min blocks

**Block A — title search (Mon):** LinkedIn search `"fractional CMO"` (rotate CFO/COO/CTO/CRO/CHRO weekly), People filter, 2nd-degree. Straight to the activity tab; look for burst-and-silence. Log 5 that qualify: name, title, the specific signal, one thing they posted you can genuinely respond to.

**Block B — watering holes (Thu):** commenters/reactors on fractional-community content (livelier prospects than post authors); people who comment on *your* posts and fit the title profile (warmest source); recent "going fractional" / "fractional practice" announcement posts. Log 5.

**The tracker (10 min/week):** one sheet — Name · Title · Source · Signal · Rung · Last touch · Verbatims heard · Outcome. The metric is *conversations opened* and *soft reveals delivered*, never connection count.

### The 6-rung ladder (DM behavior + pipeline stages, unified)

**Global rules:** never skip a rung. Never pitch before rung 4. Every message written fresh from their actual content — the examples below are *registers to hit*, not scripts. If a message would survive being pasted to 50 people unchanged, it's wrong. One follow-up max per rung; silence after that = park them (stay a warm commenter, no more DMs).

**R1 — Engage (days 1–7).** 2–3 substantive comments on their posts across a week (this is the shared 15-min routine). A qualifying comment adds a datapoint, an opinion, or a scar. Never mentions Voyce.
> *Register (on a fractional CFO's messy-handoff post):* "The 'first 30 days is archaeology' framing is right. What surprised me building for fractional folks is how many say the *second* engagement is harder — the first client came from their network, and the network only goes one layer deep."

**R2 — Connect (day 5–10, after they've seen your name twice).** Request with a ≤300-char note referencing the specific exchange. No pitch, no link, no mention of what you're building. If they engaged back on a thread first, a blank request is fine — the thread is the note.
> *Register:* "Your line about inheriting three dashboards and zero definitions has been rattling around my head since Tuesday. I write about how consultants keep inbound alive — figured our feeds should overlap properly."

**R3 — Converse (within days of accept).** One genuinely curious question about *their* posting rhythm or pipeline — real customer discovery. Reference the burst-and-silence you actually saw. Do not mention the product even if it hurts.
> *Register:* "Can I ask you something I'm asking a lot of fractional execs lately — not a pitch, I'm doing research. I noticed you had a great run of posts in April and then things went quiet. When client work picks up, is posting the first thing that drops? Trying to work out whether people who go quiet stop because of time, or because the blank composer is just miserable."

Then *listen*. Capture exact words in the tracker — these verbatims are the manifesto's missing evidence base. A dry pipeline, an unjustifiable ghostwriter, or "I know I should post more" = the rung-4 trigger.

**R4 — Soft reveal (only after they've voiced the pain themselves).** Honest agenda confession + preview-mode offer. The ask is *feedback/interview*, not purchase. Preview mode is the whole close — it answers "what if it posts something wrong under my name?" before they ask.
> *Register:* "OK, confession — I do have an agenda, just not the one you think. I'm building a tool for exactly what you described: it finds the day's news in your niche, writes your take in your voice, and actually hits publish while you're with clients. Every post logged with its source, one-click pause, official LinkedIn API — the same door Buffer uses. I'm looking for 10 people to poke holes in it before I harden the copy. Want me to run it in preview mode against your profile? You'd see exactly what it would post — nothing goes live unless you arm it."

**R5 — Preview trial.** Voyce armed on their profile in preview/dry-run for a week; Rishav does the setup. Optionally send 1–3 sample posts in *their* voice first — "sounds like me" is the sale. Treat as interview #N of 10.

**R6 — Paid ask (never before a week of previews).** Founding-member close: $49/mo (billed as ₹4,199), founding price locked, no auto-renewal. Record verbatim answers to the two validation questions ("would you pay $49?" / "would you allow it unattended?"). If "not for me": ask why (research gold), thank them, stay a commenter.

**Voice discipline across all rungs:** the language bank — showing up, going quiet, the blank composer, burst-posting, while you're with clients, sounds like me, pipeline. Never: automate your LinkedIn, content at scale, zero-touch, AI-generated content, game-changing, or any stat you can't cite. No "Hope this finds you well." No exclamation points doing enthusiasm's job.

### Who NOT to DM (ICP §4 + operational adds)

- **Loves writing / wants an editor** — autonomy is a bug to them
- **Personal-story-heavy creators** — news-reaction autonomy will underserve them and they'll say so publicly
- **Employed anyone** (corporate execs, salespeople, SDRs) — compliance kills unreviewed posting; the budget holder is the employer
- **Agencies** — "not yet" until the approval queue ships; log for later, don't ladder
- **Recently posted an anti-AI-content rant** — persuadable in theory, screenshot risk in practice
- **Consistent, happy posters** — no pain; tell them to keep their copilot
- **Competitors, tool-builders, growth-hackers** asking about engagement automation — hard no
- **Anyone who didn't accept or didn't reply after one follow-up** — parked, permanently un-DM'd, still warmly commented on

## 4. Pipeline scoreboard — filled every Friday, 30 min (~0.5 h/week + 10-min organic check)

**The whiteboard rule:** every row ladders to pipeline (₹) or it doesn't get a row. Impressions, likes, and follower counts are not on the board — screenshot them for build-in-public posts, then ignore them. The outcome metric Voyce owns is **client conversations booked** (ICP §3; manifesto word bank: "pipeline / client conversations — never impressions").

**Bookkeeping rules:** one tracker row per human, with a Rung column. A conversation only counts if it's 2-way and about *their* problem — "thanks for connecting" is not a conversation. Pipeline ₹ uses 40% (midpoint of the 30–50% trial→paid range) until 10 real data points replace it.

### Kill / double-down rules (read top-down; fix the *highest broken rung*, never add volume or a channel below a broken rung)

**Double-down triggers**
1. **R3 reply rate ≥50% and conversations converting to reveals** → the question works; raise R2 requests toward (never past) the cap of 5/week.
2. **Samples/previews land ≥50% "sounds like me"** → make the sample the reveal: send one voice-matched post *with* the R4 confession. Codify with `/money-skillify`.
3. **Any single post drives ≥2 inbound conversations** → that rotation archetype goes 2×/week in the next calendar; screenshot the chain (post → DM → call) as a build-in-public post.
4. **One watering hole produces >half of accepts/conversations** → drop the weakest, double time in the winner.

**Change-the-input triggers (fix X, not volume)**
5. **Accept rate <40%** → R1 engagement is too thin — slow down, comment more before connecting. (<20% = list targeting is off; re-screen against the 3 ICP criteria.)
6. **R3 reply rate <30%** → the question smells like a pitch; rewrite against the language bank (ICP §5). Do NOT send more of the same.
7. **Conversations happen but reveals decline (R4→R5 <30%)** → trust objection: lead with preview/kill-switch language ("nothing goes live unless you arm it") earlier.
8. **Previews shown but trials/asks refused (R5→R6 <30% over 5+ asks)** → the existential quality question (ICP §6.3). Stop outreach 2 days, fix voice-matching on your own profile, run the 14-post reach test. Bad samples burn the list.
9. **Trials but no conversions** → read the verbatims, don't guess. "Won't allow unattended" dominates → approval queue becomes build priority #1 and "one-tap approve" the headline (manifesto §6.1's pre-ratified pivot). Price dominates → revisit $49 with data (manifesto §6.4).
10. **4 weeks of posts → 0 conversations AND 0 profile-view lift** → change the *content mix*, not the count: shift toward honest-metrics posts (the only format with a documented post→customer chain) and the meta-dogfooding reveal format. 5 posts/week is the ceiling — more posts is never the answer.
11. **100+ comments → <10 ICP accepts** → change *rooms*, not comment count. You're commenting where consultants aren't.
12. **Reveal-gate miss (Day 12 gate fails twice)** → the feed isn't earning the confession; pause the reveal sequence, run the 14-post reach test before proceeding.

**Kill triggers**
13. **Global kill:** 4 weeks of honest execution (≥80% of leading targets hit) and still <3 conversations and 0 trials → the problem is ICP or message, not effort. Stop, finish the 10 customer interviews, re-open `icp-research`. Cranking volume against a falsified message is how founders burn 6 months.
14. **Vanity kill:** any week you catch yourself optimizing G1 or screenshotting like-counts as progress → that week's review starts from rule 13's question. Impressions alone are a decision to feel busy instead of selling.
15. **Product-tinkering kill:** if leading metrics (L3–L7) are <50% of target while any build work shipped that week, the scoreboard is red regardless of what the product looks like. Distribution hours come first.
